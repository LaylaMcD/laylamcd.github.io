An Overview of the Coverage of Cultural Heritage Sites in Morocco

Description

This ArcGIS map provides a brief overview of cultural heritage sites in Morocco recommended for further research across Wikipedia and Manar al-Athar platforms. Despite being recognized as UNESCO World Heritage sites, some of these areas suffer from a lack of research. Adding more detail and information about these sites will help in establishing a chronology that pays attention to historic Moroccan sites outside of Roman archaeology and increases the scope of research on Moroccan cultural heritage.

Coastal Sites

These sites can be found all along the Western coast of Morocco, facing out towards the Atlantic. The more Northern cities show distinct signs of Spanish influence, from their proximity to the Iberian peninsula.

Middle Region

Not quite by the desert, and far from the ocean, these sites still played an important role as intermediary post stations along the routes to trade and shipping ports on the coast.

Desert Sites

These sites are primarily found at the edges of the Sahara desert. Many are key trade sites for camel caravans preparing to make the long journey through the desert.
